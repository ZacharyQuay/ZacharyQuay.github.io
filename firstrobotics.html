FIRST stands for For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology. The FIRST Robotics Competition is a nationwide competition that challenges teams of young people, with adult mentors, to solve an engineering design problem in an intense and competitive way. While solving problems, team members learn life skills such as self-confidence, communication, and leadership. FIRST teams are challenged in the areas of science, engineering, and technology.

On my robotics team, I have the job of fabricating parts for the robot on two different CNC machines, a router and a mill, and a manual lathe. Most of the work is done on the router cutting aluminum brackets. To make aluminum brackets, 0.09 inches of flat aluminum stock was used. The first operation was creating pilot holes that will be finished with a drill press. The next step is to cut out the profile where we used a one flute endmill. The mill is used to make a few plates and other various pieces that required heavier operations.
