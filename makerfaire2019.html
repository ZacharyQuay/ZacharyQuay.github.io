Maker Faire 2019

For 2019, I created an amateur radio TV (ATV) base station (well, sort of). The transmission was over amateur radio bands, the 70cm, but the transmission was kept at such a low power that a clear picture could not be received any more than ten feet. I would have required extra equipment to transmit at a farther distance. Here are weekly progress updates I wrote on how the project is turning out and what I am doing.

 

3 Weeks Before Maker Faire

This year for Maker Faire, I decided to create an amateur radio TV base station. The end goal is to be transmitting pictures in real time over the 70cm ham bands. I was inspired to do this project because it struck me as interesting since I can be transmitting real-time video over the ham bands. Amateur radio has come a long way from Morse code and voice. So far, I feel that I am managing my time well and my project is progressing so far, and I have only had one short hiccup so far. Right now, I have collected some facts and information that will be used in the slideshow that I will be transmitting. These facts include information about amateur radio TV, the science behind it, how radios work, and a few other topics I am thinking of adding. Next week I plan on working and getting most of the slideshow done. My project is not super hard, but it is not easy. I have found a balanced project that is reasonably challenging that works well with my schedule.

 

2 Weeks Before Maker Faire

I have been making progress on creating slides for my presentation. I am finalizing the information and I will be done with the slides by next week. I will be testing and adjusting the transmitter I have so my TV can receive a clear signal. I must find out if I need specific antennas or not for the TV and transmitter. My project is progressing just fine, but it appears that will take longer than I expected. I will, though, have enough time to complete my project when maker faire comes around.

 

1 Week Before Maker Faire

This week I finished up the information that my slides will have. What I must do is create the actual slides. I have not been managing my time that well because I still must make two working antennas for the TV and the transmitter, so the transmission can be received. My project is progressing but not as well as I expected it to. I will be able to get my project done if I work on it at home.

 

Here is the bash script that put the images into the frame-buffer

		
			#!/bin/bash

			ARGC=$#

			if [ $ARGC -eq 1 ]; then

				IMGDIR=$1

				INTERVAL=5

				fbi -noverbose -a -t $INTERVAL -u 'find $IMGDIR -iname "*.jpg"'

			else

				echo "Usage:" $0 ""

			fi
